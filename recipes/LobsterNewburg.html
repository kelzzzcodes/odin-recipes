Lobster Newburg

Description

Lobster Newburg is a rich seafood dish that's thickened with egg yolks and cream and spiked with sherry, cognac, or Madeira.

According to legend, it originated at Delmonico's Restaurant in New York City in the late 1800s.

Ingredients

  • Cream and egg yolks: This decadent lobster Newburg starts with a mixture of heavy cream and egg yolks.
  • Butter: You can use butter or margarine.
  • Sherry: You can use dry sherry or Madeira.
  • Seasonings: This lobster Newburg is seasoned with salt, cayenne pepper, and nutmeg.
  • Lobster: You'll need cooked lobster meat, broken into chunks.

Steps

  1. Whisk the cream and egg yolks.
  2. Melt the butter, stir in the cream mixture and sherry, and cook until thick.
  3. Remove from the heat, season, and stir in the lobster.
  4. Cook the lobster mixture until it's heated through.
